# Early-Stage Creator Onboarding

Use this when a creator agrees to test the platform. The goal is not polished self-serve onboarding yet; the goal is to help each creator publish one strong release, share it with their audience, and give useful feedback.

## Positioning

You are joining an early pilot for a platform that turns digital art releases into collectible printed booklets for subscribers.

- You keep ownership of your artwork.
- You publish curated releases for collectors to follow.
- We handle the platform, subscription flow, printing, and fulfillment.
- In return, we ask for direct feedback and one real audience-share test.

## Before The Setup Call

Ask the creator to prepare:

- Artist name and preferred public profile URL slug.
- A short bio for collectors, ideally 2 to 4 sentences.
- Links to the places where they already share art.
- 5 to 10 finished artworks that feel cohesive together.
- PayPal email for payouts, if they are ready to provide it.

## Setup Steps

1. Create the creator profile.
   Use the creator setup flow to add display name, profile slug, bio, and existing platform links. Keep the bio clear and collector-facing rather than overly formal.

2. Add payout details.
   Payout setup can be skipped during the first session, but explain that it must be completed before money can be paid out.

3. Upload initial artworks.
   Start with at least 5 strong pieces. Prefer a coherent theme over a random portfolio dump.

4. Create the first release.
   Give the release a simple title, short description, and a clear theme. The first release should be easy for the creator's existing audience to understand in one sentence.

5. Preview the public creator page.
   Check that the profile, artworks, release copy, and links make sense to someone who has never heard the pitch before.

6. Share the referral link.
   Ask the creator to post once in a channel where their audience already pays attention, such as Instagram stories, Discord, newsletter, DeviantArt, ArtStation, or a link-in-bio update.

## First Release Guidance

A good first release should be:

- Cohesive: one visual mood, subject, or series.
- Print-friendly: high-resolution images with enough detail to work on paper.
- Easy to explain: one title and one short promise for collectors.
- Audience-relevant: close to the type of work followers already like from the creator.

Avoid asking early creators to overproduce. The first release is a learning artifact, not a final commercial launch.

## Suggested Launch Copy

Short bio or story copy:

```text
I'm testing a new way to turn my digital art into printed booklet releases. If you want to follow the first drop and help shape the pilot, you can subscribe here: {{CREATOR_URL}}
```

Post caption:

```text
I'm trying something new: a curated print booklet release built from my digital art. The platform handles printing and delivery, and I'm using this first run to learn what collectors want. Follow the release here: {{CREATOR_URL}}
```

Discord or newsletter:

```text
I'm part of an early pilot for a platform that turns digital art releases into printed booklets for subscribers. I uploaded my first release and would love feedback from people who already follow my work. You can view it here: {{CREATOR_URL}}
```

## Feedback To Capture

During or after onboarding, record:

- Was the creator able to explain the product back in their own words?
- Which setup step caused confusion or hesitation?
- Did they have enough suitable artwork ready?
- Did payout setup feel too early, too late, or unclear?
- Which sharing channel did they choose and why?
- What objections did they expect from their audience?
- What would make them comfortable promoting the release more than once?

## Success Criteria

Treat onboarding as complete when:

- Creator profile is published.
- At least 5 artworks are uploaded.
- One release is created or ready to create.
- Creator has previewed their public page.
- Creator has their share link and one approved message.
- Founder has captured feedback and next action.

## Follow-Up Cadence

- Same day: send profile link, launch copy, and the specific next action.
- After 48 hours: ask whether they shared it and what reactions they saw.
- After 7 days: review profile views, signups, subscriptions, and qualitative feedback.
- Before the next cycle: confirm whether they want to publish another release or adjust the first one.
